Week 3: Walking Robot

Design Challenge

The core design challenge for this week was to design a robot that could walk 10 feet in a roughly straight line, using up to four servos. The central problem was how to break the "symmetry of motion", and allow the robot to reset its walk cycle without resetting any forward progress it had made.

General Approach

Our general approach was modeled after a modified version of the "feed dogs" on a sewing machine. The basic idea was that we had two pairs of feet, movement feet and elevator feet. The movement feet would effectively slide horizontally, pushing the robot forward. The elevator feet would lift the robot into the air, allowing the movement feet to reset without pushing the robot backwards.

Daily work

Day 1

Day 1 was highly productive, but focused on brainstorming. We came up with our general robot design, then started constructing the chassis and feet. The starting construction was cardboard, to decrease prototyping time and allow for easier construction. We got the movement servos mounted, and worked on basic code to test with. There was also work done on 3d modeling the design and digitally testing if the movement cycle would work. It looked like it would have tight tollerances, but be functional.
